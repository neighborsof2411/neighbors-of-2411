## Background

The California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) requires that public agencies
disclose and analyze the significant environmental impacts of a project,
consider feasible alternatives, and adopt feasible mitigation measures. Our
concern is that the environmental review for this project **[understates
impacts / relies on outdated baseline data / fails to adequately analyze

## Talking Points

- The DEIR's baseline conditions **[do not reflect current traffic counts /
  air monitoring data / other]**, understating the project's true impact.
- The alternatives analysis fails to adequately evaluate a **[reduced
  intensity / alternative site / alternative use]** alternative capable of
  reducing significant impacts.
- Proposed mitigation measures for **[traffic / air quality / noise]** are
  vague, unenforceable, or improperly deferred to a later date (a common CEQA
  deficiency — mitigation must be specific and enforceable at the time of
  approval).
- Cumulative impact analysis does not adequately account for other approved
  or pending projects in the area: **[list nearby projects, if known]**.
- Request that the agency prepare a **[Supplemental / Subsequent] EIR** or
  substantially revise and recirculate the DEIR to address these deficiencies.

>
> I believe the DEIR is deficient in the following respects:
>
> [Insert 1-3 specific, substantive comments — reference DEIR section numbers

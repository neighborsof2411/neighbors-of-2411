## Talking Points

- The traffic study's trip-generation assumptions [may / do not] reflect
  [comparable facilities / peak seasonal demand / e-commerce growth patterns].
- Truck routes pass within **[distance]** of **[school/park name]**, raising
  child pedestrian safety concerns during school arrival/dismissal hours.
- Intersection(s) **[name]** are already at capacity / have a documented
  collision history; the project would add **[XX]%** more trips.
- Proposed mitigation measures (e.g., signal timing changes, turn restrictions)
  [do not / may not] adequately address the projected impact.
- Request a full, independent traffic study reviewed by a qualified third party.

## Template Letter

> Dear [Agency/Commission Name],
>
> I am writing regarding the proposed [Project Name] at 2411 [Street Name]
> (Case No. [XX-XXXX]). I am concerned about the project's traffic and safety
> impacts on our neighborhood, specifically:
>
> [Insert 1-3 personal, specific points — your street, your commute, your
> children's school route, a near-miss you've witnessed, etc.]
>
> I ask that the [Planning Commission / City Council] require a comprehensive,
> independent traffic and safety analysis before any approval, and that the
> project be denied or substantially modified if it cannot demonstrate that
> neighborhood streets and intersections can safely absorb this level of truck
> and vehicle traffic.
>
> Sincerely,
> [Your Name]
> [Your Address]
